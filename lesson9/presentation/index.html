The purpose of a good site layout is to provide a safe and economical flow of materials and people around a plant which is socially acceptable to workers and neighbors to produce a specified product reliably and cost-effectively. Site access, utility locations, soil conditions, safety and health considerations, temporary services, and the list goes on. Not only is there a huge list of factors that go into this process, but it's not uncommon for a site to change as development occurs. The site layout turns up to be an important parameter when it comes to project scheduling and execution. As the site layout is directly related to the actual site area availability and utilization of that area so that the project is carried out in smooth flow. Layout problems are found in several types of manufacturing systems. Typically, layout problems are related to the location of facilities (e.g., machines, departments) in a plant. They are known to greatly impact the system performance. Most of these problems are NP hard. I therefore strongly believe that our site plan layout is going to be absence of any hazards.
