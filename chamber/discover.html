The cloudy beautiful road leading up to the Kwahu mountains in the Eastern Region of Ghana

The Abetifi Stone Age Park was discovered by the archaeological department of the University of Ghana after conducting a successful excavation at the site.

The Afram River is a 100-kilometre river in Ghana. Prior to the construction of the Akosombo Dam in the 1960s, the Afram is a principal tributary of the Volta River and today is an equally important tributary of Lake Volta.

Kwahu Plateau, plateau, southern Ghana. It comprises the uplifted southern edge of the Volta River basin and extends for 160 miles (260 km) northwest-southeast from Wenchi to Koforidua.
